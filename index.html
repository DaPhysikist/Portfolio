Who am I?

Engineering student passionate about Embedded Systems

Hello! 😄 I’m Pranav, a Gen-Z Indian-American who grew up fascinated with construction sites, airplanes, computers, and the entire idea of inventing. Typical of most members of my generation, I grew up conscious of radical environmental changes occurring because of human activity. From a young age, I have wanted to acquire knowledge and use that knowledge to make this planet a cleaner, greener and happier place.

I am committed to finding and implementing innovative ways to combat pollution and waste through the use of science and technology, and I believe that through effective research and sustainable practices, there are real possibilities out there to discover. I am currently looking for research and internship opportunities where I can apply my knowledge of science, computer science, electrical engineering, networking, and cloud technologies.

I'm currently a second year undergraduate student at UC San Diego, where tech 💻 and natural beauty 🌸 converge. My hobbies include reading 📖, gaming on my Switch 🎮, and taking walks/jogs/scooter rides to explore 🌍 new places!

    • Developed software module using C++ and Arduino IDE for ESP8266 wireless board that records readings of anemometer sensor using analog voltage values and receives data from wind direction sensor using Modbus protocol and RS485 serial interface
    YouLostIt Project
    • Made a Bluetooth tracker device using a power-efficient real-time embedded application on a STM32 B-L475E-IOT01A board that enters “lost” mode when the board has not moved for 60+ seconds and starts sending BLE packets to nearby phones
    • Wrote bare-metal drivers in C for the GPIO and Timer peripherals, communicating with the accelerometer sensor using I2C, and communicating with the Bluetooth Low Energy chip using SPI, read more: https://www.pranavmehta.com/you_lost_it.html
    Donnez App
    • Solely developed an Android app in Kotlin that enables users to send others reward points for acts of kindness or volunteering
    • Users set up accounts with cloud-based multi factor authentication using AWS Amplify Auth (AWS Cognito)
    • Points are sent with P2P wireless communication using Android Nearby Connections and are stored in a local database that syncs to the cloud when the user connects to the internet, implemented with AWS Amplify Datastore (DynamoDB & GraphQL)
